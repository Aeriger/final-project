Though many animated films are hand drawn, animators for the most part, use some sort of software to at least assist in their animations. Some of the most popular ones are listed below.


Photoshop - As one of the most productive and popular graphic manipulation software, Photoshop is used in almost every aspect of every type of animation.


3ds Max - Often compared to Maya, 3ds Max is generally seen as the main software for video game animation and modeling, while Maya is used for films.


Unity Pro 4 - For aspiring or veteran game designers, Unity Pro 4 is often the staple engine for most video games, whether they be for simple mobile games or expansive console RPG's.


Illustrator CC - Essentially the Photoshop of vector based graphics, Illustrator essentially has a monopoly in the graphic design industry.


Toon Boom Studio - For those particularly interested in 2D animation, Toon Boom Studio is a strong contender, used in hundreds of famous shows and films.


Maya - The staple of the 3D animation industry, Maya has been used in Academy Award winning films, and includes almost everything needed in modeling, cloth, fluids, physics, and particle effects.
